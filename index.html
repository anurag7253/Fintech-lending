Understanding Your Credit Report

Your credit report is crucial for accessing financial products. Here's what you need to know.

Key Components of Credit Reports

  • Personal Information: Name, address, employment details, and identification.
  • Credit Accounts: Details of your loans, credit cards, and other debt obligations.
  • Payment History: Record of your on-time and delayed payments on all accounts.
  • Credit Inquiries: Records of when lenders have accessed your credit report.
  • Public Records: Bankruptcies, foreclosures, and other financial legal matters.

Credit Score Fraud Warnings

  • OTP Fraud: Never share One-Time Passwords with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank or credit bureau.
  • Information Theft: Don't share your CIBIL report with untrusted individuals or organizations.
  • Phishing Attempts: Be cautious of emails or calls requesting your credit information. Verify before sharing.
  • Regular Monitoring: Check your credit report regularly to detect unauthorized activities or inquiries.
  • Secure Access: Only access your credit report through official channels and secure websites.
